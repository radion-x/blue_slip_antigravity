Pink Slip FAQs

Common questions about eSafety Checks in Sydney

What is a pink slip?

A pink slip (now called an eSafety Check) is a safety inspection report required for vehicles over 5 years old to renew their NSW registration. It ensures your car complies with safety standards.

How long is a pink slip valid?

A pink slip is valid for 6 months using the electronic report. You must renew your registration within this period.

What is the difference between a pink slip and a blue slip?

A pink slip is for currently registered vehicles needing annual renewal. A blue slip is for unregistered vehicles, new interstate transfers, or vehicles with expired registration (>3 months).

How much does a pink slip cost?

The cost is regulated by the NSW Government. As of 2026, it is approximately $42‚Äì$51 for light vehicles. Motorcycles and trailers have different rates.

What if my car fails the inspection?

If your vehicle fails, you will receive a "Repairs Needed" report. If you complete the repairs and return to us within 14 days, the re-inspection is generally free.

Do I need a pink slip every year?

If your vehicle is more than 5 years old, yes, you generally need an eSafety Check every year to renew your registration. Your renewal notice will confirm this.

Do you inspect motorcycles and trailers?

Yes, we conduct eSafety checks for motorcycles and trailers. Please call ahead to book.

How long does the inspection take?

A standard eSafety check takes about 15‚Äì30 minutes.
